About

Primordial is a tabletop wargame built to land between the army-building depth of a game like Warhammer and the simplicity of Risk. Players spend points to build an army, split their troops into ranks of five, and fight fast, readable battles. The design goal: a 7–8 year old can play it, but there are still real decisions to make.

Three troop types (infantry, archers, cavalry), optional champion upgrades, and one unified combat procedure for both shooting and melee. Every roll uses a d20.

How Combat Works

On your turn your units attack and the enemy defends — only the defender loses models, then they strike back on their turn.

  • Each side rolls one d20 per model in the fight (max 5), and applies its modifiers.
  • The defender chooses how to pair their dice against the attacker’s — the key decision.
  • Each attacker die that beats its pair removes a model; extra attacker dice are automatic hits.
Design note: an early version sorted both players’ dice high-to-low, which caused all-or-nothing wipes. Letting the defender assign matchups (verified with a 150,000-trial simulation) cut the chance of a full wipe from ~50% to ~2% on a strong charge, while keeping cavalry charges meaningful.
